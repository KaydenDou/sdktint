Pro Classic >

Pro Classic is a 1.5 mil color stable deep dyed film. This film offers outstanding dye fastness and lifetime warranty against product failure or defect. Pro Classic is designed to be the film you use everyday with confidence.

C2 Carbon >

C2 is an advanced nano carbon film produced using the latest technology in the industry. Using this technology, it is a film that not only looks great, it performs great too. In addition, this process has allowed premium looks and performance at a mid-range price point.

Pro Nano Ceramic >

Geoshield Pronano Ceramic Automotive film is revolutionizing the window film business. Pronano is a next generation automotive film that fuses color stable polyester with nano ceramic particles. This hybrid combination delivers outstanding infrared heat rejection and uncompromising looks.

Apex Ultra Ceramic >

Geoshield has been specializing in ceramic window films since 2005. Over those 15 years, technology has only gotten better. The current “apex” in window film technology is this new super ceramic aptly called APEX. If you are looking for one of the highest heat rejecting films on the market. Apex is it. With over 95% IR rejection across the entire Infrared spectrum, Apex delivers outstanding heat rejection capabilities.

Faqs >

  • What are the differences between the types of films?
  • Dyed Film is used for aesthetics and uv protection making your vehicle look good, and protecting yourself and your interior from the sun, meanwhile the carbon, ceramic, and super ceramic add onto those benefits by offering different levels of heat rejection, keeping your car cool. As you go from carbon, to ceramic, to super ceramic, the amount of heat rejected increases as well.
  • Why is removal of old tint a seperate charge?
  • Due to the usage of cheap or failing tint, old tint can be very difficult to remove and sometimes takes hours. The adhesive left behind by the old tint must be removed completely or else the new tint applied will not be able to be applied. Even a tiny bit of adhesive left behind can ruin a tint job. As such, please do not remove your own tint as improper removal is difficult to fix.
  • What are your hours?
  • I am a full time student, and also have a part time job on mondays and tuesdays. We can find a time that works for both of us after receiving a quote.
  • Will my defroster lines still work after removal?
  • DISCLAIMER: There is a chance that they will be damaged during removal, I have not damaged any yet, but on very old or cheap tint, the adhesive can damage the defroster lines. Depending on the severity of the old tint, I will let you know the risk before working on it.
  • How long do you take?
  • Depending on the vehicle, as of now I usually take around 3-5 hours to finish a full vehicle. Though I am working on becoming faster.
  • Why is there clear bubbles in my film after install?
  • Due to weather conditions, water bubbles may remain in your tint until it evaporates, but after it does you cannot see it and nothing will remain. Different contamination such as dirt, air bubbles, or fibers should be discussed.
  • I have flaws in my tint
  • Any major flaws should not have gone out, but if it does, contact me and I can redo it free of charge. But, small flaws can be present as dust in the air is very hard to control and even the best shops will have small dirt specks in places. No tint job can come out completely perfect.
